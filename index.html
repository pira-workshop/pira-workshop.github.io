About the Workshop

Welcome to the Perception for Industrial Robotics Automation (PIRA) Workshop, taking place on June 12, 2025! This event brings together advanced computer vision research and practical industrial robotics. Even with many breakthroughs, solving real-world tasks like robotic bin picking is still challenging. At PIRA, we focus on topics like 3D scene understanding, 3D vision, egocentric and embodied AI, foundation models, robot learning, and synthetic data. Our goal is to connect researchers and industry experts to build more reliable, faster, and scalable industrial solutions.

Perception Challenge for Bin Picking

We invite you to join our Intrinsic Bin Picking Challenge, featuring $60k in prizes! The competition runs from February 1st to May 1st, and winners will present their solutions at the workshop.

This challenge is organized by OpenCV in collaboration with Intrinsic, BOP, and the University of Hawaii.

Invited Speakers

Vincent Vanhoucke

Waymo

Vincent is a Distinguished Engineer at Waymo, whose work spans robotics, machine learning, and visual perception. He founded and led the Robotics Team at Google and co-founded the Conference on Robot Learning. Vincent has received multiple awards and is a Fellow of the IEEE.

Kartik Iyer

TRUMPF Smart Factory

With over 20 years in the fabrication, Kartik directs the TRUMPF Smart Factory in Chicago, overseeing the integration of advanced robotics and automation. His focus is on using these technologies to transform manufacturing processes, driving efficiency and precision in real-time production.

Bowen Wen

NVIDIA

A Senior Research Scientist at NVIDIA, Bowen specializes in robotic perception and computer vision, earning accolades for his impactful research, including a best paper nomination at RSS '22. He has several impactful papers published in CVPR, RSS, ICRA.

Kartik Venkataraman

Intrinsic (Alphabet)

As Head of Perception R&D at Intrinsic, Kartik is pioneering work in plenoptic imaging, neural rendering, and multi-view approaches for industrial and consumer applications. His expertise spans computer vision, deep learning, and computational imaging, with a background that includes founding two imaging startups.
