About the Workshop

The Perception for Industrial Robotics Automation (PIRA) workshop is designed to bridge the gap between advanced computer vision research and its practical application in industrial robotics. Despite significant progress in CV, robust deployment in tasks such as robotic bin picking remains challenging.

Focusing on topics such as 3D scene understanding, 3D vision, egocentric and embodied AI, foundation models, robot learning, and synthetic data, PIRA will bring together researchers and practitioners to drive innovation in reliability, cycle time, and scalability for industrial applications.

Intrinsic Bin Picking Challenge

In collaboration with OpenCV and the BOP benchmark, the Intrinsic Bin Picking Challenge seeks to accelerate progress in 6DoF pose estimation for industrial-grade parts. Participants will develop robust solutions for robotic bin-picking pipelines, addressing real-world challenges of efficiency and accuracy.

The challenge unfolds in three phases:
Phase 1: Development – optimize algorithms using an initial dataset.
Phase 2: Vision-Only Testing – adapt solutions to new, unseen parts.
Phase 3: Robot-in-the-Loop Testing – deploy top solutions on a real robotic system.
